| [URF-Loc: Uncertainty-Aware Frequency-Spatial Network for GNSS-Denied UAV Geo-Localization](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-981-92-3504-9_20) | URF-Loc addresses cross-view geo-localization for UAVs operating under GNSS-denied conditions, where severe appearance variations, large search spaces, and ambiguous structural correspondence make robust localization difficult. The paper observes that prior approaches either rely on global retrieval with limited spatial precision or on deterministic dense prediction without modeling prediction reliability, leading to unstable localization. To address this, the authors propose a coarse-to-fine framework. The coarse stage employs a spatial-frequency joint (FSJ) representation that injects high-frequency residuals into multi-scale features to enhance view-invariant structural cues for robust global matching. The refinement stage introduces an uncertainty-aware regression (UAR) module that formulates localization as heteroscedastic probabilistic regression, enabling adaptive handling of ambiguous regions and providing reliable uncertainty estimates. Experiments are conducted on the UAV-VisLoc benchmark, where URF-Loc is reported to achieve state-of-the-art performance, significantly improving meter-level accuracy and bounding-box quality while providing reliable uncertainty estimation. Specific numerical values are not available from the provided preview text. | UAV-VisLoc | - |
| [Unifying UAV Cross-View Geo-Localization via 3D Geometric Perception](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01747v1) | This paper addresses UAV cross-view geo-localization in GNSS-denied environments, where severe geometric discrepancy between oblique UAV imagery and orthogonal satellite maps undermines robust matching. Most prior pipelines treat coarse place retrieval and fine pose estimation as decoupled stages, treating perspective distortion as appearance noise. The authors propose a unified, geometry-aware framework that reconstructs a local 3D scene from multi-view UAV sequences using a Visual Geometry Grounded Transformer (VGGT) and renders a satellite-aligned Bird's-Eye View (BEV) that serves as a geometric intermediary between retrieval and pose regression. A Satellite-wise Attention Block isolates the interaction between each satellite candidate and the reconstructed UAV scene, preventing inter-hypothesis interference while reducing computational complexity from quadratic to linear in the number of candidates. A dual validation strategy combining geometric and feature-similarity criteria selects the optimal satellite reference for 3-DoF pose refinement (latitude, longitude, heading). The authors also release a recalibrated University-Pose extension of University-1652 with explicit 3-DoF pose annotations and an IoU-based retrieval protocol. Experiments on University-Pose/University-1652 and SUES-200 report that the Refined BEV variant achieves R@1 of 79.0 on University-1652 and 68.0 on SUES-200 (150 m), substantially outperforming reported SOTA baselines while enabling meter-level positioning within retrieved tiles. | University-1652, University-Pose (recalibrated extension of University-1652), SUES-200 | [Code](https://github.com/Collebt/Uni-CVGL) |
| [Lightweight Cross-View Localization via Quantized Knowledge Distillation and Adaptive Local Feature Matching](https://doi.org/10.1109/lsp.2026.3670737) | This paper addresses the challenge of cross-view localization between aerial UAV imagery and satellite reference images, where extreme viewpoint differences make it difficult to balance retrieval efficiency, matching accuracy, and model compactness. The authors propose a lightweight two-stage framework that couples efficient global retrieval with semantically enhanced local matching. In the first stage, ternary quantized transformers are adapted to the cross-view domain through progressive transfer learning, enabling compact global image retrieval without relying on large-scale pretraining. The second stage introduces a dual-branch architecture that fuses texture and semantic features via cross-attention reasoning and applies semantic conditioning to suppress mismatches arising from repetitive structures. Experiments on public cross-view datasets demonstrate improved localization accuracy alongside reduced computational cost, and the authors highlight the framework's suitability for deployment on resource-constrained UAV platforms. The abstract does not enumerate specific dataset names, nor does it provide explicit numerical metrics or leaderboard-style results in the available evidence. | - | - |
| [GRiM-Net: A Two-Stage Cross-View Visual Localization Framework for UAVs](https://doi.org/10.3390/rs18101477) | GRiM-Net addresses GNSS-denied autonomous UAV flight by proposing a two-stage, jointly optimized cross-view visual localization framework that couples coarse global retrieval with pixel-level fine matching. The pipeline first runs a global retrieval module that aggregates deep features to rank candidate satellite-map patches from a pre-built index, narrowing localization from a full aerial map to a local region. A fine matching module then performs joint keypoint detection and description on both the UAV query and the retrieved satellite candidate, followed by bidirectional correspondence matching and weighted homography estimation that maps the image center into geographic coordinates. Both stages share a backbone with domain-adaptive batch normalization and are trained end-to-end with a triplet loss for retrieval together with keypoint, descriptor, and homography reprojection losses for matching, producing synergistic feature representations. Experiments are conducted on public urban cross-view benchmarks and show that GRiM-Net delivers efficient and robust geographic coordinate regression suitable as a practical component for broader UAV navigation systems. | [GeoRVLF: A Robust Drone-Satellite Visual Geo-Localization Framework for Small Unmanned Aerial Vehicle Platforms](https://doi.org/10.1109/lra.2025.3572821) | This letter addresses drone-to-satellite visual geo-localization for small unmanned aerial vehicles operating in GNSS-denied environments, where challenges include cross-scale heterogeneous scene matching and real-time computation under limited onboard resources. The proposed GeoRVLF framework integrates three components: (1) a Cross-scale Place Recognition (CPR) module based on a multi-level block retrieval algorithm that reduces the runtime and storage cost of feature-based visual place recognition, supported by a Heterogeneous Scene Matching network (HSM-Net); (2) a Fine Pose Registration (FPR) module that produces 4-DoF global pose estimates through alignment between drone and satellite imagery; and (3) a fusion of FPR with a visual odometry (VO) module through pose graph optimization to improve stability and operating frequency on resource-constrained platforms. The authors evaluate the framework through dataset assessments and real-world flight experiments, reporting trajectory accuracy approaching low-accuracy GPS and operating frequency comparable to classic VO methods. | [Exploring the best way for UAV visual localization under Low-altitude Multi-view Observation Condition: a Benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.10692) | This paper introduces a benchmark for UAV Absolute Visual Localization (AVL) under low-altitude (<300m) multi-view (pitch angles 20°–90°) conditions, where extreme viewpoint changes challenge conventional nadir-view methods. The authors construct AnyVisLoc, a large-scale dataset of 18,000 UAV images captured by seven DJI drone types across 15 cities, 25 regions, and multiple altitudes, with both aerial photogrammetry and historical satellite reference maps plus DSM data. A unified coarse-to-fine framework integrates image-level retrieval (template matching, NetVLAD, drone-to-satellite deep networks), pixel-level matching (handcrafted, sparse/dense learning-based), and PnP+RANSAC pose solving, evaluated under four localization strategies. A new retrieval metric PDM@K is proposed to better correlate retrieval performance with downstream localization accuracy. The best combined pipeline (CAMP retrieval + RoMa matching + Top-N Re-rank) achieves 74.1% accuracy within 5m on the aerial map and 94.2% within 20m, with satellite maps yielding only 18.5% at 5m. The authors further analyze how reference map type, pitch angle, and prior information noise (yaw/pitch/altitude) affect localization accuracy, highlighting significant room for improvement. | AnyVisLoc | [Code](https://github.com/UAV-AVL/Benchmark) |
