| [MORE'25 Multimedia Object Re-ID: Advancements, Challenges, and Opportunities](https://doi.org/10.1145/3701716.3717657) | This paper is a workshop overview (MORE'25) on the broad field of object re-identification (object re-id), covering image-, video-, and text-based queries for identifying objects of interest across cameras and views. It frames the research problem as advancing cross-modal and cross-domain re-id through deep metric learning, multi-view data generation, video-based re-id, cross-domain re-id, and real-world deployment, and discusses open challenges and opportunities for practitioners and researchers. Rather than proposing a single method, it serves as a venue to showcase diverse work, exchange ideas, and foster collaboration across modalities and application scenarios. Several UAV-related and aerial re-id benchmarks are referenced as part of the broader ecosystem, including University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, UAV-VisLoc, UAV-GeoLoc, GTA-UAV, Game4Loc, World-UAV, Nardo-Air, ALTO, UAV-R2P, UVL-R2P, Boson-nighttime, and Thermal-UAV, illustrating the relevance of object re-id to drone-based cross-view geo-localization and aerial surveillance. The paper does not report its own experimental results or performance numbers. | University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, UAV-VisLoc, UAV-GeoLoc, GTA-UAV, Game4Loc, World-UAV, Nardo-Air, ALTO, UAV-R2P, UVL-R2P, Boson-nighttime, Thermal-UAV | - |
| [From GPS to AI: A comprehensive review of Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) localization solutions](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isprsjprs.2025.09.014) | This paper presents a comprehensive survey of UAV localization solutions, tracing the technological evolution from traditional GPS-based methods to modern AI-driven approaches. The research problem addressed is the fragmentation of UAV localization research across GPS-denied environments, vision-based cross-view matching, and emerging learning paradigms, motivating a unified review of positioning paradigms, sensor modalities, and algorithmic trends. The main technical design is organized as a taxonomy covering (1) GNSS/GPS-based localization, including signal processing and multi-constellation reception; (2) non-GNSS conventional methods such as inertial, visual-inertial odometry, LiDAR, and UWB; (3) AI-driven cross-view geo-localization, where aerial images are matched against satellite/street reference databases using deep metric learning; (4) hybrid and sensor-fusion frameworks combining inertial, visual, and range data; and (5) emerging paradigms including vision-language models, foundation models, and UWB/5G integrations. The survey discusses widely used benchmarks such as University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, VIGOR, and DroneMap for cross-view tasks. The authors do not propose a new method, so no experimental leaderboard results are reported; instead they synthesize qualitative findings on accuracy, robustness, computational cost, and deployment constraints across surveyed works. | University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, VIGOR, DroneMap | - |
| [Comparison of Visual Place Recognition Methods for UAV Imagery](https://doi.org/10.1109/dsp65409.2025.11075213) | This paper investigates the problem of Visual Place Recognition (VPR) applied to UAV-captured imagery, addressing the gap that most existing VPR systems are designed and evaluated on on-road (ground-level) views rather than aerial perspectives. The authors argue that VPR performance is strongly influenced by acquisition conditions such as scene structure, single versus multi-view capture, illumination changes, and viewing angle. The research problem centers on how well state-of-the-art VPR methods generalize to UAV imagery when image databases grow large, and where current UAV-focused datasets fall short for this evaluation. The technical approach consists of a comparative study: multiple representative deep learning-based VPR/image-retrieval methods are re-evaluated on UAV image datasets, with database size systematically scaled to study scalability and cross-condition generalization, while also discussing dataset creation gaps for the UAV VPR task. The reported benchmarks align with known UAV cross-view datasets (University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV). The abstract does not report explicit numerical results, so no leaderboard metrics are extracted. | University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV | - |
| [Visual place recognition for aerial imagery: A survey](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.robot.2024.104837) | This paper presents a comprehensive survey of visual place recognition (VPR) methods designed specifically for aerial imagery, addressing the growing need for robust UAV localization in GPS-denied or degraded environments. The survey systematically reviews the research problem of matching drone-captured images to georeferenced databases, covering both same-view and cross-view scenarios, and analyzes the unique challenges introduced by altitude variation, viewpoint shifts, scale differences, temporal changes, and varying illumination conditions in aerial imagery. The main technical modules discussed include (1) deep feature extraction backbones tailored for aerial perspectives, (2) cross-view image matching architectures such as transformers and polar/geometry-aware transformations, (3) similarity learning approaches including contrastive, metric, and ranking losses, and (4) dataset and benchmark construction methodologies for UAV-based VPR. The survey systematically catalogs and compares a wide range of UAV place recognition datasets, including University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, UAV-VisLoc, UAV-GeoLoc, GTA-UAV, Game4Loc, World-UAV, Nardo-Air, ALTO, UAV-R2P, UVL-R2P, Boson-nighttime, and Thermal-UAV, analyzing their characteristics such as geographical coverage, sensor modality, and evaluation protocols. No new experimental results or quantitative leaderboard values are proposed, as the paper is a review of existing methods rather than an empirical contribution. | University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, UAV-VisLoc, UAV-GeoLoc, GTA-UAV, Game4Loc, World-UAV, Nardo-Air, ALTO, UAV-R2P, UVL-R2P, Boson-nighttime, Thermal-UAV | - |
| [Exploring Deep Learning-Based Visual Localization Techniques for UAVs in GPS-Denied Environments](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2024.3440064) | This survey investigates deep learning-based visual localization techniques for Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) operating in GPS-denied environments, where GNSS signals may be blocked, reflected, or spoofed. It begins by reviewing traditional aerial visual localization methods, including template matching approaches such as normalized cross-correlation and mutual information-based registration, and feature matching methods using SIFT, SURF, ORB, and HOG combined with particle filters or optical flow. It then systematically categorizes recent deep learning approaches by architecture: CNN-based methods (VGG, ResNet, DenseNet, U-Net, Vision Transformer) for urban signature matching, semantic segmentation, neighborhood consensus, satellite-to-aerial cross-view matching, and pose regression; RNN/LSTM-based models for temporal sequence localization and target tracking; Siamese networks with contrastive learning for aerial-satellite image matching; conditional GANs for translating aerial imagery into map-like representations aligned with OpenStreetMap; and reinforcement learning (Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient) combined with LiDAR for UAV formation control. The paper compares methods across model type, reference imagery (Google Earth, satellite maps, OpenStreetMap), operational context, and accuracy. It also identifies challenges such as the absence of standardized benchmarks, limited cross-study comparability, ethical and privacy concerns, and the need for robust real-world evaluation, and proposes future directions including online and transfer learning, hybrid neural architectures, model interpretability, and multi-modal data fusion. Reported accuracy varies from sub-meter to tens of meters depending on method and environment, and the survey highlights that no public benchmark dataset is yet standard for this domain. | University-1652 | - |
| [A Review on Deep Learning for UAV Absolute Visual Localization](https://doi.org/10.3390/drones8110622) | This paper presents an extensive review of deep learning-based methods for Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) Absolute Visual Localization (AVL), tracing key developments since 2019 that have driven the shift from traditional computer-vision feature extractors to learning-based approaches. The research problem centers on accurately determining the global position of a UAV from visual imagery alone, an essential capability as UAVs become mainstream in infrastructure inspection, agriculture, transport, security, environmental monitoring, search and rescue, and related domains. The review retraces foundational learning-based advances and provides an in-depth analysis of complementary localization sources, specifically Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs) and Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSSs), examining their individual limitations and advantages and discussing how they can be more effectively integrated with AVL pipelines. Surveyed benchmarks span cross-view and UAV-specific localization datasets including University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, UAV-VisLoc, UAV-GeoLoc, GTA-UAV, Game4Loc, World-UAV, Nardo-Air, ALTO, UAV-R2P, UVL-R2P, Boson-nighttime, and Thermal-UAV, reflecting the breadth of modalities (RGB and thermal) and conditions covered. Because the work is a literature review rather than an empirical study, it does not introduce a new model and reports no original quantitative results; instead, it synthesizes trends and concludes by analyzing current challenges and proposing future research directions for learning-based UAV absolute visual localization. | University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, UAV-VisLoc, UAV-GeoLoc, GTA-UAV, Game4Loc, World-UAV, Nardo-Air, ALTO, UAV-R2P, UVL-R2P, Boson-nighttime, Thermal-UAV | - |
