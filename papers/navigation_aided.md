| [Združevanje vizualnega prepoznavanja lokacij in metode SLAM s histogramskim filtrom za robustno vizualno lokalizacijo](https://openalex.org/W7139212661) | This bachelor thesis targets robust UAV localization in GNSS-denied environments, where Visual Place Recognition (VPR) alone gives globally consistent but ambiguous and noisy per-image place estimates, while visual SLAM/odometry supplies locally accurate frame-to-frame displacement without a global reference. The proposed system combines a VPR front-end based on AnyLoc (DINOv2 patch features aggregated with a VLAD vocabulary, producing cosine-similarity probability heatmaps over satellite tiles) with a visual SLAM/odometry front-end that yields relative distance and yaw between consecutive frames. These cues are fused inside a discrete Bayesian histogram filter operating on a discretized satellite map: the prediction step convolves the previous belief heatmap with a banana-shaped kernel that jointly encodes distance and orientation uncertainty (line kernel times ring kernel), and the update step multiplies the predicted heatmap element-wise with the new VPR heatmap, followed by normalization. Evaluation is performed on the UAV-VisLoc benchmark (9 of 11 maps used, excluding one with insufficient valid samples and one with very large map size), reporting Absolute Position Error (mean, median, min) and Recall@Cells / Recall@Distance. Across the reported tables, the filter substantially reduces VPR-only errors (e.g., Map 1 mean APE from 853.35 m to 67.57 m, median from 450.49 m to 64.90 m; Map 4 mean APE from 1153.77 m to 85.64 m, median from 340.20 m to 81.20 m), with the weakest performance on coastal Map 8 (mean 1860.49 m, median 433.01 m). | UAV-VisLoc | - |
| [Towards GNSS-Denied Geo-Positioning Using Search Area Refinement](https://doi.org/10.5220/0014620800004084) | This work tackles the problem of UAV geo-positioning when GNSS is unavailable or unreliable, a critical capability for maintaining autonomous flight during satellite-denied operations. The authors propose two cross-view geo-localization pipelines that constrain matching to a refined search area. The first method, FVL-SAR, combines a fully convolutional network with a deep neural network to perform coarse-to-fine matching between UAV imagery and overhead reference data. The second method, TVL-SAR, employs an attention-only Vision Transformer for the same cross-view matching task. To produce continuous state estimates between visual fixes, an Extended Kalman Filter propagates the UAV pose and dynamically narrows the search area, although the authors observe accuracy degradation through long feature-sparse stretches. The methods are evaluated on a subset of UAV-VisLoc and on the full 106.19 km VPAIR trajectory, which the paper reports as the first published flight-through mean error results for both datasets. On UAV-VisLoc single measurements, FVL-SAR achieves 32.39 m on 62.36% of images while TVL-SAR reaches 39.44 m on 67.31%. Under EKF integration, mean errors grow to 61.00 m for FVL-SAR and 79.53 m for TVL-SAR. | UAV-VisLoc, VPAIR | [Code](https://github.com/xdKazer/P7 Drone Geolocalization) |
| [Towards UAV Localization in GNSS-Denied Environments: The SatLoc Dataset and a Hierarchical Adaptive Fusion Framework](https://doi.org/10.3390/rs17173048) | This paper addresses precise and robust localization of micro Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) in GNSS-denied environments, where existing visual matching approaches underperform in real-world conditions and suitable benchmarks are scarce. To tackle these issues, the authors introduce the SatLoc dataset, a new multi-source benchmark with synchronized sensor data across diverse real-world scenarios designed for UAV-to-satellite image matching. They also propose SatLoc-Fusion, a hierarchical localization framework that combines three complementary layers: absolute geo-localization from satellite imagery using DinoV2, high-frequency relative motion tracking from visual odometry with XFeat, and velocity estimation via optical flow. An adaptive fusion strategy dynamically weights each layer's output based on real-time confidence metrics, producing a self-consistent state estimate. Deployed on a 6 TFLOPS edge computer, the system is reported to run in real time at over 2 Hz, with absolute localization error below 15 m and trajectory coverage exceeding 90%, described by the authors as state-of-the-art. Overall, the work provides both a dataset and a comprehensive baseline for advancing UAV navigation without GNSS. | SatLoc | - |
| [Particle Filter-Based Localization Using Visual Feature Synchronization in GNSS-Denied Navigation](https://doi.org/10.1109/ICNS65417.2025.10976891) | This paper addresses autonomous UAV navigation in GNSS-denied environments, where reliance on inertial navigation systems (INS) causes drift and where direct matching between onboard camera imagery and satellite reference imagery is hindered by significant domain discrepancy. To overcome these issues, the authors propose a vision-aided localization framework that couples a CycleGAN-based domain adaptation module with a Marginalized Particle Filter (MPF). CycleGAN is used to translate UAV onboard images into a satellite-like visual domain so that feature correspondences with reference satellite imagery become more reliable, while the MPF fuses these visual feature matches with the INS prediction to refine state estimates and suppress drift. The work does not specify any public cross-view benchmark dataset, instead focusing on real-world UAV flight tests under various visual domain conditions, reporting robustness of trajectory estimation, reliable feature alignment across domains, and computational feasibility for onboard use, without providing quantitative leaderboard metrics. | - | - |
| [Visual Localization system for GPS-Blind Environments in Unmanned Aerial Vehicles](https://doi.org/10.1109/icct62929.2024.10875019) | This paper addresses UAV visual localization in GPS-denied or GNSS-failure scenarios, where reliable backup navigation is needed for safe autonomous flight. The authors propose a Visual Place Recognition-Based Navigation Subsystem targeted at high-altitude operation without accurate initial coordinates, framing vision as a substitute for satellite navigation. The technical pipeline is built around visual place recognition, combining aerial image matching with localization refinement so that the UAV can recover its position from onboard camera data when GNSS is unavailable or compromised. To support evaluation, the paper introduces the AeroVL dataset, which spans more than 80 km² of diverse terrain including urban areas, farmlands, and forests. The authors validate the pipeline through extensive testing on AeroVL, demonstrating precise positioning under GPS-denied conditions. No public leaderboard of tracked benchmark datasets such as University-1652, SUES-200, DenseUAV, or UAV-VisLoc is reported; AeroVL is a new dataset introduced here. Reported results are described qualitatively in terms of effective localization across challenging landscapes, without explicit numeric leaderboard tables. | AeroVL | - |
